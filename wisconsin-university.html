Wisconsin university

The University of Wisconsin–Stout is a public university in Menomonie, Wisconsin. A member of the University of Wisconsin System, it enrolls more than 9,600 students. The school was founded in 1891 and named in honor of its founder, lumber magnate James Huff Stout. Stout is "Wisconsin's Polytechnic University".
